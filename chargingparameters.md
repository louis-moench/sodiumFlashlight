In https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/stamp/stamp.jsp?tp=&arnumber=11033803, researchers observed severe degradation of sodium-ion batteries when overcharged to just 4.3V:
"the SIB overcharged to 4.3 V reaches the end-of-life standard in terms of available capacity after only five cycles."

While most li-ion charge ICs cut off around 4.2V, charging to 4.2V is close enough that it has a good chance of reducing the battery's useful charge cycle count. For that reason I will pursue a charger that cuts off at 4.0V.
